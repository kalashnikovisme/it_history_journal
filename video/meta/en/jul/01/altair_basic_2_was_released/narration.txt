July 1 in the History of Computer Science: On this day in 1975, MITS began shipping Altair BASIC 2.0—the first commercial software product from the young company then called Micro-Soft. Written by Bill Gates, Paul Allen, and Monte Davidoff, this BASIC interpreter made programming accessible to Altair 8800 owners, establishing commercial software for microcomputers. It marked the beginning of an industry that would soon transform personal computing.

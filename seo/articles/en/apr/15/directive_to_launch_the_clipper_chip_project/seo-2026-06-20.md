**FAQ Block:**
Q1: What was the Clipper Chip?
A1: The Clipper Chip was a hardware encryption module launched in 1993 by the U.S. government, intended to provide secure communication with built-in access for authorities.

Q2: Why was the Clipper Chip controversial?
A2: It was criticized for violating cryptography principles, creating a single point of compromise, and enabling government backdoors, raising privacy concerns.

Q3: What principles did the Clipper Chip violate?
A3: It violated the principles that security should not depend on secrecy of algorithms and that systems should not have single points of compromise.

Q4: When did the Clipper Chip project end?
A4: The project lost support by 1996, as opposition grew among cryptographers and tech companies.

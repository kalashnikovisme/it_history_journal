**FAQ Block:**

Q1: What is Lexis and why is it significant?  
A1: Lexis was the first search engine for legal information, launched in 1973, revolutionizing legal research by enabling quick access to case law and statutes.

Q2: How did Lexis work in its early days?  
A2: It connected terminals to mainframes via telephone lines, allowing users to input queries and receive search results from magnetic tapes stored data.

Q3: What was the cost of using Lexis?  
A3: Usage costs ranged from $100 to several hundred dollars per hour, depending on the database accessed, reflecting the high costs of early computing.

Q4: What is Nexis and how is it related to Lexis?  
A4: Nexis, introduced in 1979 by Mead, focused on news and business data. It later merged with Lexis to form LexisNexis, a leading legal and business research platform.

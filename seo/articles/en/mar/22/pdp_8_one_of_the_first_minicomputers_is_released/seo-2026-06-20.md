**FAQ Block:**  
Q1: What was the significance of the PDP-8?  
A1: The PDP-8 was one of the first affordable minicomputers, making computing accessible to universities, labs, and industries, and influencing future designs.  

Q2: Who developed the PDP-8?  
A2: It was developed by Digital Equipment Corporation (DEC), founded by Ken Olsen and Harlan Anderson.  

Q3: How did the PDP-8 differ from earlier computers?  
A3: It was smaller, cheaper, and easier to use, with a 12-bit architecture and simplified instruction set, unlike large mainframes.  

Q4: What legacy did the PDP-8 leave?  
A4: It paved the way for interactive computing and influenced subsequent computers like the PDP-11 and modern architectures.

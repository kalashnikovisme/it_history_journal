**FAQ Block:**
- **Q1:** Why was the HP-35 a milestone in calculator history?  
  **A1:** It was the first scientific pocket calculator capable of advanced functions like logarithms and trigonometry, fitting in a shirt pocket.
- **Q2:** How did the HP-35 influence science and engineering?  
  **A2:** It replaced slide rules, enabling faster, portable calculations, and set the stage for digital calculators.
- **Q3:** Why was the HP-35 expensive at launch?  
  **A3:** Its advanced technology and compact design made it a premium device, costing $395 in 1972 (~$2,800 today).
- **Q4:** What is Reverse Polish Notation (RPN)?  
  **A4:** A calculation input method that allowed faster computation without an equals button, used by the HP-35.

**FAQ Block:**
Q1: What is SCCS?
A1: SCCS (Source Code Control System) was the first widely distributed version control system, created at Bell Labs in 1977 to manage source code revisions.

Q2: Why was SCCS important?
A2: It introduced structured revision tracking, change comments, and efficient storage, laying the groundwork for modern version control tools.

Q3: How did SCCS influence later systems?
A3: SCCS's concepts of delta storage and revision history influenced systems like RCS, CVS, Subversion, and Git.

Q4: Who developed SCCS?
A4: SCCS was developed by Marc J. Rochkind at Bell Labs, where Unix and many foundational computing innovations originated.

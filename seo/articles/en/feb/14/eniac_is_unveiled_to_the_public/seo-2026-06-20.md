**FAQ Block:**
Q1: What was ENIAC and why is its unveiling on February 14, 1946, significant?  
A1: ENIAC was the first general-purpose electronic digital computer, and its public unveiling marked the start of the modern computer era.

Q2: Who designed and built ENIAC?  
A2: It was designed by John Presper Eckert and John Mauchly, with a team of engineers, technicians, and six pioneering women programmers.

Q3: How powerful was ENIAC?  
A3: It weighed 30 tons, contained 18,000 vacuum tubes, and could perform 5,000 additions per second, revolutionizing calculations.

Q4: What impact did ENIAC have on future computers?  
A4: It demonstrated the practicality of electronic computation, influencing subsequent machines like EDVAC and UNIVAC I.

Q5: Was ENIAC easy to program?  
A5: No, programming involved manual rewiring and was complex, but it paved the way for more flexible, programmable computers.

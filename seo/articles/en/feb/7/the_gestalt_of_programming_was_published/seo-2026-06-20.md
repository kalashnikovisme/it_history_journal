**FAQ Block:**
Q1: What is Gestalt Programming?  
A1: It’s a concept introduced by Douglas Ross in 1956, emphasizing expressing programming intentions as whole ideas rather than step-by-step instructions.

Q2: Why was Gestalt Programming considered radical?  
A2: Because it proposed a human-centered approach, allowing direct expression of ideas to computers, predating interactive and graphical computing.

Q3: How does Gestalt Programming influence modern computing?  
A3: Its principles underpin high-level languages, visual programming, and AI-assisted development, focusing on idea-based interaction.

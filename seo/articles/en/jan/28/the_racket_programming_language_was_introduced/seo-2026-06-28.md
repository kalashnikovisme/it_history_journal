**FAQ Block:**

Q1: What is Racket?  
A1: Racket is a programming language developed in 1995, derived from Scheme, designed for language creation, macros, and metaprogramming.

Q2: Why was Racket created?  
A2: To provide a flexible platform for building new languages and experimenting with language design, especially for educational and research purposes.

Q3: How does Racket differ from other Lisp dialects?  
A3: Racket allows users to reshape syntax and semantics via advanced macros and language-building tools, making it highly customizable.

Q4: Who uses Racket today?  
A4: Racket is used in academia, industry (e.g., Naughty Dog for game tools), and for technical publishing with Scribble.

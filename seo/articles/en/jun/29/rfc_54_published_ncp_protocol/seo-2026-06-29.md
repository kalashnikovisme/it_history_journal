A1: RFC 54 is a document published in 1970 that contained the first complete draft of the Network Control Protocol (NCP), a foundational networking protocol.  

Q2: Why is RFC 54 significant?  
A2: It marked the beginning of an open, community-driven process for developing Internet standards, leading to the protocols we use today.  

Q3: How did RFC 54 influence modern networking?  
A3: It established the practice of requesting comments and collaborative review, shaping the development of protocols like TCP/IP.

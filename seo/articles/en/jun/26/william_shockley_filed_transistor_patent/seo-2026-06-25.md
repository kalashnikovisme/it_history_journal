**FAQ Block:**

A1: William Shockley was a physicist at Bell Labs who filed the patent for the junction transistor in 1948, a key invention in electronics.

Q2: What is a junction transistor?  
A2: A junction transistor is a semiconductor device that amplifies or switches electronic signals, foundational to modern microelectronics.

Q3: Why was the invention of the transistor important?  
A3: It replaced bulky vacuum tubes, enabling smaller, faster, more reliable, and energy-efficient electronic devices.

Q4: How did Shockley's patent influence modern technology?  
A4: It laid the groundwork for integrated circuits, microprocessors, and virtually all modern electronic devices.

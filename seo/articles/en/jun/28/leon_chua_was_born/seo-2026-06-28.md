**FAQ Block:**

Q1: Who is Leon Chua?  
A1: Leon Chua is an American electrical engineer and professor known for his pioneering work in circuit theory and the invention of the memristor.

Q2: What is a memristor?  
A2: A memristor is a passive circuit element whose resistance depends on the history of electrical charge passed through it, retaining its state even after power is off.

Q3: Why is the memristor important?  
A3: It has the potential to revolutionize memory storage, AI accelerators, and in-memory computing, enabling faster and more efficient electronic devices.

Q4: When was the memristor first proposed?  
A4: Leon Chua proposed the concept in 1971, and practical devices matching the theory were developed decades later in 2008.

Q5: What are some of Leon Chua's other contributions?  
A5: Besides the memristor, he developed Chua's Circuit demonstrating chaos in electronics and Cellular Neural Networks for fast image processing.

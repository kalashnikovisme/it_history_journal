**Title:** June 19, 1999 — The Release of the Very First Counter-Strike

*The story behind the mod that revolutionized multiplayer gaming.*

On June 19, 1999, the first public beta of Counter-Strike was released—a Half-Life mod that would go on to influence countless multiplayer shooters. Created by enthusiasts Minh Le and Jess Cliffe, the mod introduced team-based, objective-driven gameplay that differed from the arena shooters of the time.

This early version featured two teams, one life per round, weapon purchases, and voice communication—elements still central to the game today. Its timing was perfect, coinciding with the rise of internet access and LAN gaming, making it accessible and popular worldwide.

In 2000, Valve recognized its success and hired the creators, leading to the official release of Counter-Strike 1.0 as a standalone product. Today, it remains a cornerstone of competitive gaming.

# June 19, 1999 — The Release of the Very First Counter-Strike

On June 19, 1999, the first public beta of Counter-Strike was released, marking a significant moment in gaming history. Developed as a mod for Half-Life by enthusiasts Minh Le and Jess Cliffe, it introduced innovative team-based, objective-driven gameplay that would shape future multiplayer shooters.

This early version featured core elements still recognizable today: two opposing teams, one life per round, weapon purchases, and voice communication—all within a mod that was freely available to Half-Life players.

The success of this mod eventually led Valve to hire its creators and release Counter-Strike as a standalone product in 2000, illustrating how grassroots projects can influence the industry.

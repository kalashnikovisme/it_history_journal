# The Quiet Importance of Unix Pipes

Unix pipes turned small command-line programs into composable tools. Instead of designing one large application for every task, programmers could connect small utilities and pass text between them.

That idea shaped shell culture, software engineering practice, and later ideas about modular systems. Pipes also made terminals feel less like fixed command launchers and more like programmable environments.

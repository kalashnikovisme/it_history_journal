May 19, 1955 — James Gosling Was Born

May 20

James Gosling is considered the main creator of Java, but his career included many other important achievements. Still, in terms of impact on both the industry and the world, Java is unquestionably his greatest accomplishment.

Before Java, he was working on completely different things. In the 1970s, Gosling studied at the University of Calgary and later joined Carnegie Mellon University, where he worked with Unix systems and compilers. There, he created Gosling Emacs — his own implementation of Emacs for Unix. Many programmers first came across his name as the author of developer tools rather than as the creator of a programming language.

In 1984, Gosling joined Sun Microsystems. At the time, Sun was one of the most engineering-driven companies in Silicon Valley. They built powerful Unix workstations and actively promoted the idea of network computing.

The story of Java began in the early 1990s inside the Green Project. Gosling’s team was trying to build a platform for smart consumer electronics: televisions, set-top boxes, and other devices that were expected to operate through networks. To make that possible, they needed a language that would run consistently across different hardware.

That led to Oak, which was later renamed Java.

Java’s central idea in the mid-1990s sounded almost magical: Write Once, Run Anywhere. Write a program once — and run it across different operating systems through the JVM (Java Virtual Machine).

Interestingly, Gosling himself always came across more as a calm engineer than a stereotypical Silicon Valley celebrity CEO. He kept writing code, working on architecture, and spoke often about the importance of engineering culture.

After Oracle acquired Sun Microsystems in 2010, Gosling left the company. He spoke quite openly about not liking the new corporate environment.

After leaving Sun/Oracle, he worked at Google on cloud technologies, then became Chief Software Architect at Liquid Robotics — a company developing autonomous ocean robots. Later, Gosling joined Amazon Web Services.

In 2024, James Gosling announced that he was retiring.
